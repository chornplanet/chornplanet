# Drone.md

**DNA Name:** DroneDNA
**Version:** Initial
**Official Standard (Refined & Standardized)**

---

## 1. Purpose

To define the official autonomous aerial mobility DNA of Chorn, ensuring all drone systems are aerospace-grade,
structurally realistic, safety-redundant, infrastructure-compatible, and governance-aligned.

DroneDNA governs structural configuration, safety architecture, branding placement, integration standards, and visual
representation discipline.

DroneDNA must remain engineering-first, non-speculative, and institutionally credible across government, investor,
aerospace, and smart city contexts.

---

## 2. Scope

This DNA applies to all Chorn aerial mobility outputs, including but not limited to:

* Human-scale drone taxi systems
* Autonomous aerial mobility vehicles (AAM)
* Urban drone corridor integration
* Infrastructure-supported drone networks
* Public aerial mobility demonstrations
* Drone-integrated smart mobility systems

Structural drone rules are governed exclusively under DroneDNA.

Ground mobility remains under **SmartMobilityDNA**.

---

## 3. Foundational Doctrine

### 3.1 Aerospace-Grade Engineering Principle

All drone systems must:

* Follow real aerospace structural logic
* Demonstrate plausible lift mechanics
* Maintain center-of-gravity realism
* Reflect material feasibility
* Respect propulsion physics

Sci-fi levitation, anti-gravity assumptions, or fantasy propulsion are strictly prohibited.

---

### 3.2 Safety-Redundancy Doctrine

DroneDNA assumes:

* Multi-layered redundancy systems
* Fail-safe architecture
* Emergency landing capability
* Power system backup logic
* Sensor redundancy

Zero-risk or accident-proof claims are prohibited.

Automation must remain accountable and override-capable.

---

### 3.3 Infrastructure-Compatible Integration Rule

Drone systems must:

* Operate within defined aerial corridors
* Integrate with SmartMobilityDNA
* Remain compatible with SmartCityDNA
* Respect MediaSmartCityDNA communication discipline
* Avoid independent uncontrolled airspace narratives

Drone mobility must remain governable.

---

## 4. Structural Configuration Standard

### 4.1 Rotor Configuration (Non-Negotiable Rule)

DroneDNA allows:

* **Quadcopter configuration only**
* Exactly **4 rotors**
* Symmetrical **X-configuration**

Prohibited:

* Tricopter designs
* Hexacopter variations
* Dual-rotor helicopter style
* Decorative or non-functional rotors

Structural symmetry is mandatory.

---

### 4.2 Passenger Configuration

Standard human-scale drone taxi must:

* Accommodate **4 passengers**
* Reflect realistic human seating dimensions
* Maintain structural cabin integrity
* Demonstrate visible entry/exit logic

Single-seat cinematic drones are not compliant unless explicitly defined for non-taxi use.

---

### 4.3 Structural Realism Requirements

All drones must:

* Show landing gear logic
* Maintain proportional rotor-to-body scale
* Reflect aerodynamic body shaping
* Avoid exaggerated entertainment styling

Vehicles must appear manufacturable under aerospace standards.

---

## 5. Branding & Logo Governance Standard

### 5.1 Canonical Logo Placement Rule

Primary manufacturer logo must be placed:

* On the **side fuselage mid-section**
* Between front and rear doors
* On a **clean white panel**
* Beneath the cabin glass
* At eye-level visibility

Logo placement must not interfere with:

* Airflow
* Sensors
* Structural seams
* Safety markings

---

### 5.2 Logo Style Standard

Logo must be:

* Gold
* Matte or brushed finish
* Non-glossy
* Flush/OEM integrated with fuselage surface
* Non-sticker-like

Branding must appear aerospace-manufacturing integrated.

---

## 6. System Architecture & Integration

### 6.1 Autonomous Governance Model

Drone autonomy must be framed as:

* AI-assisted
* Infrastructure-coordinated
* Regulation-compatible
* Human-override capable

Prohibited:

* Fully independent AI dominance narratives
* “Pilotless perfection” claims
* Zero-failure statements

---

### 6.2 Energy & Power Discipline

Drone systems must demonstrate:

* Electrification alignment
* Battery system plausibility
* Thermal management logic
* Power redundancy layers

Energy claims must remain measurable and non-promotional.

---

### 6.3 Corridor & Airspace Integration

Drone mobility must include:

* Defined aerial corridor logic
* Urban clearance consideration
* Landing pad integration
* Coordinated air traffic systems

Floating free-flight city swarms are prohibited.

---

## 7. Visual & Representation Standards

### 7.1 Engineering Realism Requirement

All visual outputs must:

* Respect physics
* Reflect real material finishes
* Maintain scale accuracy
* Avoid fantasy city backdrops
* Avoid exaggerated futuristic glow effects

DroneDNA is not cinematic science fiction.

---

### 7.2 Institutional Presentation Rule

Drone outputs must be suitable for:

* Government transport review
* Aerospace investor presentations
* Urban infrastructure feasibility studies
* Smart mobility exhibitions
* Aviation research documentation

Hype-driven visual dramatization is prohibited.

---

## 8. Ethical & Compliance Boundaries

DroneDNA must avoid:

* Militarized drone framing
* Weaponized configurations
* Political symbolism
* Aggressive dominance narrative
* Fear-based air accident imagery
* Competitive performance exaggeration

Drone mobility must remain civic-focused and infrastructure-aligned.
