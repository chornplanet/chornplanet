# Sword.md

**DNA Name:** SwordDNA
**Version:** Initial
**Official Standard (Refined & Standardized)**

---

## 1. Purpose

To define the official ultra-luxury royal ceremonial sword DNA of Chorn, establishing strict standards for form, proportion, materials, craftsmanship, structural logic, and regal visual authority.

SwordDNA governs blade geometry, hilt architecture, connector discipline, sheath integration, material hierarchy, and representation standards.

SwordDNA must remain craftsmanship-first, heritage-respectful, materially credible, and free from fantasy exaggeration.

---

## 2. Scope

This DNA applies to all Chorn sword-related outputs, including but not limited to:

* Royal ceremonial swords
* Ultra-luxury heritage blades
* Regal presentation weapons (non-combat functional framing)
* Sheath and belt integration systems
* Close-up craftsmanship documentation
* Institutional exhibition presentations

SwordDNA governs the sword object itself.
Character styling and wardrobe remain governed by **LannaDNA** when applicable.

---

## 3. Foundational Doctrine

### 3.1 Regal Authority Principle

All SwordDNA outputs must convey:

* Calm authority
* Structural dignity
* Balanced proportion
* Composed power

The sword represents sovereignty and discipline — not aggression.

---

### 3.2 Craftsmanship-First Doctrine

Every sword must appear:

* Handcrafted or master-forged
* Materially authentic
* Structurally plausible
* Mechanically coherent

Decorative excess without structural logic is prohibited.

---

### 3.3 Non-Fantasy Discipline Rule

Prohibited:

* Glowing magical blades
* Oversized “anime-scale” proportions
* Impossible edge thickness
* Floating components
* Sci-fi hybrid weapons

SwordDNA must remain physically forgeable under real metallurgy constraints.

---

## 4. Blade Architecture Standard

### 4.1 Blade Geometry

Blade must demonstrate:

* Proportional taper from base to tip
* Clean central axis alignment
* Edge realism (sharpenable geometry)
* Balanced thickness gradient
* Controlled curvature (if curved design)

Blade must not appear brittle, decorative-only, or structurally weak.

---

### 4.2 Material Discipline

Acceptable blade materials:

* High-carbon steel
* Damascus-pattern steel (subtle, not exaggerated)
* Polished tempered steel

Prohibited:

* Chrome-like mirror exaggeration
* Fantasy crystal textures
* Unreal glowing veins

Surface finish must reflect metallurgy realism.

---

### 4.3 Blade-to-Handle Connector (Royal Connector Standard)

The connector (Habaki-inspired element) must:

* Transition cleanly between blade and guard
* Demonstrate precision machining or hand-forged fit
* Reflect structural reinforcement logic
* Use premium metal (e.g., brushed gold, polished steel)

Loose, floating, or decorative-only connectors are prohibited.

---

## 5. Hilt & Grip Governance

### 5.1 Handle Structure

Handle must:

* Reflect ergonomic logic
* Maintain proportional grip diameter
* Align with blade axis
* Support two-hand or single-hand configuration intentionally

Oversized theatrical grips are not allowed.

---

### 5.2 Material Hierarchy

Acceptable grip materials:

* Fine leather wrap
* Premium ray skin (subtle)
* Structured cord wrapping
* Carved hardwood core

Luxury elements (gold, engraved metal) must:

* Reinforce structure
* Avoid excessive ornament clutter
* Remain regal, not flamboyant

---

### 5.3 Pommel Discipline

Pommel must:

* Balance blade weight visually
* Reflect structural counterweight logic
* Integrate seamlessly with grip

Detached decorative spheres or fantasy spikes are prohibited.

---

## 6. Guard (Tsuba-Inspired or Crossguard) Standard

Guard must:

* Protect hand logically
* Maintain proportional scale
* Reflect cultural refinement without imitation copying
* Avoid exaggerated spikes or weaponized extensions

Guard design must remain ceremonial-elegant, not combat-aggressive.

---

## 7. Sheath (Scabbard) Integration Standard

### 7.1 Structural Fit

Sheath must:

* Fit blade geometry precisely
* Align with blade curvature
* Demonstrate insertion logic
* Avoid floating gap visuals

Loose-fit cinematic sheath depiction is prohibited.

---

### 7.2 Material Standard

Acceptable sheath materials:

* Lacquered hardwood
* Premium leather
* Brushed metal accents
* Subtle engraved details

Finish must remain refined and institutionally presentable.

---

### 7.3 Carrying System (Belt & Strap Rule)

If strap or belt is included:

* Must be high-quality leather
* Structurally supportive
* Proportionally accurate in length
* Mechanically attachable to sheath hardware

Decorative-only ribbon attachments are prohibited.

---

## 8. Visual & Representation Standards

### 8.1 Ultra-Luxury Presentation Rule

SwordDNA must convey:

* Museum-grade presentation
* Controlled lighting
* Material texture clarity
* Premium macro detailing

Over-dramatic battle scenes are prohibited.

---

### 8.2 Background Discipline

Acceptable contexts:

* Neutral studio
* Heritage architectural setting
* Regal ceremonial environment
* Institutional exhibition space

Prohibited:

* Battlefield chaos
* Violent conflict scenes
* Blood depiction
* Aggressive combat narrative

SwordDNA represents authority, not violence.

---

## 9. Ethical & Compliance Boundaries

SwordDNA must avoid:

* Militarized propaganda framing
* Political symbolism
* Nationalistic dominance messaging
* Violent intimidation narrative
* Fear-based authority projection

The sword must represent discipline, heritage, and refinement.

---

If inscriptions are present:

* Non-ideological
* Non-aggressive
* Structurally engravable

No slogans, no propaganda text, no combat claims.
